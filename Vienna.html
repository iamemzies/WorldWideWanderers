Vienna

Hello! Monika here and welcome to Vienna! As the capital city of Austria, the city offers visitors a rich history as the city of the monarchy and abundant historical buildings and museums. Fly into Vienna International Airport. The airport is located just outside the city limits of Vienna and the City Airport Train (CAT), connects the airport with the transport hub at Vienna Central in only 16 minutes. Getting around is easy, choose from rail, underground, trams and buses. The underground will take you to within a few minutes of walk anywhere you are likely to visit. Bring a comfortable pair of shoes, as walking is an easy way of getting around, especially in the inner city. What's there to see? The city offers a variety of museums and buildings. Here are some examples:

Schoenbrunn Palace

This is the former residence of the imperial family and is a World Cultural Heritage site. Of the palace's 1441 rooms, 40 are open to the public; the Imperial Tour takes you into 26 of these. Visitors will find magnificent gardens and these harbour the world's oldest zoo, the Tiergarten, founded in 1752; and a 630m-long hedge maze.

St Stephen's Cathedral

The cathedral is the symbol of Vienna and is one of the most important Gothic structures in Austria. From the exterior, the first thing that you will notice is the glorious tiled roof, with its Austrian eagle. Inside, the valuable altars and side chapels can be viewed, with relics decorated with gold and precious stones. Numerous noblemen and archbishops have been laid to rest in the catacombs of the cathedral.

Belvedere Palace

Here you can see Gustav Klimt's famous painting 'The Kiss' and other major works from Monet and van Gogh. This Baroque landmark comprises of the Upper and Lower Belvedere and an extensive garden.

Embrace the city centre, have some lunch and walk down the narrow walkways to discover the city.
